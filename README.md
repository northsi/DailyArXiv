### Superconductivity (19 new today)

Today's research on superconductivity spans fundamental theory, materials engineering, and quantum device applications. A prominent theme is the exploration of unconventional pairing mechanisms and topological properties, with several studies focusing on chiral d+id'-wave order, Fermi-arc models, and finite-momentum pairing in systems like the α-𝒯₃ lattice, cuprates, and magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene. Methodologically, there is a strong emphasis on advanced numerical techniques, including constrained-path quantum Monte Carlo for benchmarking correlations and time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau simulations for diode effects, alongside analytical exact solutions for strongly correlated models. In applied quantum technology, significant efforts are directed at engineering superconducting circuits and resonators, optimizing qubit coherence through surface loss quantification, and developing novel elements like double-junction circuits and multilayer heterostructures to control nonlinearities, diode behavior, and spin coupling. Concurrently, thermal management in accelerator magnets and fabrication methods for nanowires highlight the ongoing materials science challenges in sustaining superconducting states for large-scale and hybrid devices.

### spin glass (9 new today)

Today's research on spin glasses spans a diverse range of systems and methodological approaches, highlighting the field's evolution beyond classical magnetic disorder. A prominent theme is the exploration of quantum spin glass phases and their connections to other complex states, such as in bosonic systems with off-diagonal disorder or in models coupling Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev fermions to a glassy landscape, where novel phases and critical properties are analyzed. Methodologically, there is a strong focus on quantum computational techniques, including the development of qubit-efficient variational algorithms for optimization and studies of quantum annealing's efficacy in reaching low-energy states in mean-field models. Experimentally and theoretically, interest remains in identifying and characterizing glassy behavior in real materials, such as cluster glass states in intermetallic Ce compounds and ferromagnetic spin glass phases in topological semimetals, often linked to anomalous transport phenomena. Concurrently, advanced analytical tools, like semiclassical expansions and perturbative loop expansions around Bethe lattice solutions, are being employed to probe the nature of excitations and critical exponents in finite-dimensional models directly at zero temperature.
