### Superconductivity (20 new today)

Today's research highlights a strong focus on engineered superconducting heterostructures and devices, where quantum confinement, proximity effects, and asymmetric designs are leveraged to create novel functionalities such as the superconducting diode effect (SDE) and enhanced spin coupling. Methodologically, there is a clear trend combining advanced theoretical frameworks—like generalized Eliashberg, time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau, and exactly solvable models—with precise material fabrication and characterization to probe both fundamental mechanisms and practical performance limits. Notable findings include the identification of many-body and vortex-driven origins of nonreciprocal critical currents, direct spatial correlations between pseudogap and superconducting dynamics in cuprates, and strategies to mitigate quasiparticle losses and improve interface quality in quantum circuits. Concurrently, significant efforts are directed toward quantum simulation and computation, utilizing superconducting platforms to study correlated electron dynamics, optimize qubit performance, and explore topological superconductivity.

Notable findings include new analytical approaches to critical exponents in a field, the identification of distinct dynamical regimes for spin waves in 2D, and innovative quantum algorithms designed to overcome hardware limitations for optimization, collectively advancing both fundamental understanding and potential applications.
